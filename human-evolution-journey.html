Human Migration Map

How Homo sapiens spread from Africa to populate every continent on Earth.

Out of Africa
~60,000–70,000 years ago, modern humans began migrating out of Africa through the Arabian Peninsula into Asia.
Reaching Europe
~45,000 years ago, Homo sapiens entered Europe, eventually displacing Neanderthals over thousands of years.
Into Australia
~50,000 years ago, humans reached Australia via a southern coastal route, becoming the ancestors of Aboriginal Australians.
The Americas
~15,000–20,000 years ago, humans crossed from Siberia to Alaska via the Bering land bridge and spread throughout the Americas.

"To understand who we are, we must first understand where we came from."

Human evolution is the lengthy process of change by which people originated from apelike ancestors. Scientific evidence shows that the physical and behavioral traits shared by all people originated from apelike ancestors and evolved over a period of approximately 6 million years.

One of the earliest defining human traits, bipedalism — the ability to walk on two legs — evolved over 4 million years ago. Other important human characteristics, such as a large and complex brain, the ability to make and use tools, and the capacity for language, developed more recently.

Genetic Evidence

DNA analysis has confirmed that all modern humans share a common ancestor who lived in Africa approximately 300,000 years ago. We share 99.9% of our DNA with each other.

Fossil Record

Thousands of fossils document the transformations in our lineage. Each discovery adds pieces to the complex puzzle of human origins.

Behavioral Evolution

The development of symbolic thinking, language, and culture represents perhaps the most significant leap in human evolution, enabling cumulative knowledge across generations.

Ongoing Research

Paleoanthropology continues to reshape our understanding. New species are still being discovered, and ancient DNA analysis is revealing unexpected connections.
